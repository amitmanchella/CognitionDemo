Side projects are the secret weapon of every great developer. They're how we learn new technologies, explore ideas, and sometimes even build businesses.

## Why Side Projects Matter

Every successful product I've shipped started as a side project. Here's why they're so valuable:

- **Low stakes experimentation** - Try new things without pressure
- **Portfolio building** - Show what you can do, not just what you've learned
- **Passion-driven learning** - Motivation comes naturally
- **Real-world experience** - Face actual problems, not textbook scenarios

## My Process

Over the years, I've developed a process that works:

### 1. Start Small

The graveyard of abandoned side projects is filled with overly ambitious ideas. Start with something you can finish in a weekend.

### 2. Solve Your Own Problem

The best side projects solve a problem you personally have. You'll understand the requirements deeply and stay motivated.

### 3. Ship Early

Don't wait until it's perfect. Ship a basic version and iterate based on feedback.

### 4. Set a Deadline

Without a deadline, projects expand forever. Give yourself two weeks and see what you can build.
